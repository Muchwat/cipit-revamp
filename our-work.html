Our Mission and Work

The Centre for Intellectual Property and Information Technology Law (CIPIT) conducts evidence-based research on issues at the intersection of law, technology, and innovation in Africa. We aim to be the leading African centre for evidence-based research and policy on law and technology, advancing approaches that prioritise human rights, local perspectives, and inclusive innovation.

Intellectual Property and Innovation

Our research provides in-depth analysis and insights into how IP frameworks can support innovation while balancing the rights of creators and the public.

Learn More →

Artificial Intelligence

We study the development and legal frameworks of AI tools and applications in Africa, focusing on their ethical implications and governance.

Learn More →

Data Governance and Policy

We provide independent analysis to support data-related policy development, focusing on data protection, privacy, and open data for the Global South.

Learn More →

Cyber Law and Policy

Our work explores the legal and policy dimensions of cybersecurity, digital forensics, and information security to create secure digital environments.

Learn More →

Digital Identity

We conduct research on the appropriate use of digital identity systems to ensure they are secure, inclusive, and respect human rights.

Learn More →

Capacity Building

We are committed to nurturing the next generation of African experts through training programs, fellowships, and mentorship initiatives.

Learn More →


The Centre for Intellectual Property and Information Technology Law (CIPIT) is an evidence-based research and training Centre based at Strathmore University, Nairobi, Kenya.
